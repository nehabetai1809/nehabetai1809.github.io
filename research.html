Automation without Borders: The Impacts of Generative AI on International Service Outsourcing

with Maggie Chen

The outsourcing of routine cognitive work to developing countries has been a resilient component of globalization. This paper investigates how generative artificial intelligence (AI) reshapes cross-border service outsourcing by tracing firm and worker responses to the diffusion of AI in a global market for digital services. Using granular transaction data linking clients, workers, and skills from 2022–2024, together with a novel capability-based measure of AI automation exposure spanning 2,700 skill categories, we document a sharp contraction in cross-border outsourcing in highly exposed skill areas. Outsourcing volume falls by roughly 35 percent, with declines concentrated in developing-country destinations that previously specialized in codifiable work. These aggregate effects mask substantial reorganization. Firms consolidate remaining outsourcing into fewer, larger, and more complex contracts. Exposed workers complete fewer contracts but earn more per engagement, as a subset reallocates toward AI- and interaction-intensive tasks. Adjustment is uneven, leading to a 22-percentage-point increase in within-skill concentration of activity. A task-based trade framework rationalizes these patterns by showing how generative AI compresses cross-border wage and productivity arbitrages in routine tasks, shifting comparative advantage and redistributing gains from trade in digital services.
